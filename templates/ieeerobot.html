IEEE Competition Robot

In Fall 2025, I got the opportunity to be the embedded software engineer for the Space Cowboys team at Oklahoma State University. The objective of the robot was to autonomously pick up randomly scattered along the 4 x 8 feet field randomly with 18 Geodinium and 16 Nebulite, the robot had to place these ores into cosmic shipping containers (bins), the beacon is to be placed in the beacon holder, and the robot must identify via apriltags where to place the bins.

I was the sole embedded software engineer on the project and I built the computer vision on a Raspberry Pi and a camera module. I developed using OpenCV and Python to identify the ores through the color purple and the apriltags. I programmed the motors to move the robot. The servos were also programmed to run the combine system that picked up ores and the the movement of the claw that grabbed the bin and the beacon. The robot also used an encoader to relay back the speeds of the motors to the Raspberry Pi.

The mechanical engineering students built the combine system for the robot.

The claw that captures the bins and the beacon.

That experience developed my leadership and programming skills immensely. As part of a team, I helped pick out the components such as the driver module, and servo driver and made sure there was enough votlage and current for every system and where I had to step down the voltage. I collaborated with mechanical who built each subsystem and designed how the robot would fit together. I also collaborated with an electrical engineer who designed the power system. One challenge I ran into was the fact that the driver module we bought was suited for Arduino and its library was written in C++ not Python that was used for our computer vision. I had to translate the C++ Library into Python which tested my skills as a developer. In that moment I displayed my conflict resolution skills.
