03 : About me

Education

I am a Bachelor's of Science in Computer Engineering alumna, with a strong interest in digital design layout and embedded systems. My academic and professional experiences have centered around hardware design, digital systems, and low-level programming, particularly in FPGA-based computing and microcontroller applications.

Job Experience

In 2024, I completed an internship at the Helmerich Research Center at Oklahoma State University (OSU), where I worked on synthesizing diamond films for use in quantum computing applications. This experience allowed me to explore materials science and its intersection with next-generation computing technologies.

Personal Work

Outside of my academic and research work, I am passionate about robotics. In my free time, I enjoy building robots using Raspberry Pi, integrating sensors, motors, and embedded software to create autonomous systems. My projects often involve wireless communication, sensor fusion, and real-time processing, bridging the gap between hardware and software. I am always eager to learn and take on new challenges in the fields of digital layout, embedded systems, and hardware design.
