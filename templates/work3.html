003 : Raspberry Pi Hackathon

In Spring of 2023 I was a Special Event Coordinator for the Urban Coders Guild. Urban Coders Guild is a Tulsa-based nonprofit that provides free, high-quality computer science education to Black, Latino, Indigenous, and female-identifying middle and high school students.

I worked for Urban Coders Guild where I project managed 12 volunteers and mediated the emotions of 35 middle schoolers during the Black Futures Hackathon therefore I know the importance of guiding someone in all situations.

That experience developed my leadership and communication skills. I also had to interact with parents which directly flows with the role of ensuring excellent community service. In the Hackathon, the Raspberry Pis wouldn’t connect to the wifi so I quickly debugged them to make sure the event went smoothly. In that moment I displayed my conflict resolution skills. Smooth operations of events are deeply important to me.

The volunteers test building the robot to prepare for the event.
